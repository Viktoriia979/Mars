Mars:

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance, which is caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It is about half the size of Earth and has a thin atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide. Mars has a cold and dry climate, with average temperatures around -63°C (-81°F), although temperatures can vary greatly depending on the season and location.

Mars rotates on its axis in about 24.6 hours, making its day very similar in length to an Earth day. It takes about 687 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun. The planet has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, and it does not have any rings. Its surface features include giant volcanoes, deep valleys, polar ice caps, and vast deserts. Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the Solar System, and Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyon systems known..

Mars is named after the Roman god of war. Scientists are especially interested in Mars because evidence suggests that liquid water once flowed on its surface, raising the possibility that the planet may have supported life in the distant past. Numerous spacecraft, rovers, and orbiters have been sent to explore Mars and learn more about its history and environment.
